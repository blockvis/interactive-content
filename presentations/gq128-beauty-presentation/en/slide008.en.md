## Full paragraph (abstract translation)

The talk concludes with the **first public presentation of GQ128** — a complex floating-point format whose **mantissa** lives in $\mathbb{Z}[\omega]$ rather than in $\mathbb{Z}$. This yields an **isotropic** quantization error — a property that is **structurally impossible** on the square lattice of the standard IEEE 754 complex representation.

**All concepts are demonstrated through interactive visualizations generated by AI in real time, without traditional slides.**

## What's important to know about GQ128

- **Base.** $Z = 3 + \omega$, $|Z|^2 = 7$. Consistent with the septenary system of slide 6: the same geometry, but in floating-point form.
- **Mantissa.** 42 slots, 3-bit digits. The number $42 = \varphi(7^2)$ is no coincidence; it's exactly what ensures self-consistency of the exact **98-point NTT** over $\mathbb{Z}/343\mathbb{Z}$ used for lossless multiplication.
- **Exponent.** Not fixed, but a "floating END marker" — a spiral coordinate with step $\arg(Z) \approx 19.1°$, irrational with respect to $\pi$.
- **Arithmetic.** Addition is **carry-bounded** via a 343-element LUT; multiplication by $\omega$ is a **cyclic shift**; negation is *two's complement* in Doppler-EGA encoding.
- **Serialization.** Values are laid out in memory along the Gosper curve — the same curve that bounds the fractal on slide 7. Locality on the curve = proximity in the $\mathbb{C}$ plane.

## Why "isotropic error" matters

On the square lattice $\mathbb{Z}[i]$, the quantization error has a different scale along the axes and along the diagonals: factor $\sqrt{2}$ in the corner, factor $1$ on the axis. This makes IEEE 754 complex **anisotropic**: the error depends on the argument. On $\mathbb{Z}[\omega]$, the six nearest neighbors are equidistant — **the error is the same in all directions**.

## GQ128's place in the hierarchy of sphere packings

GQ128 is the 2D member of a broader family "kissing number + 1 = number of digits", which we unfold separately in the discussion:

| $d$ | Lattice | $\kappa$ | $p = \kappa + 1$ | Format |
|-----|---------|----------|------------------|--------|
| 1 | $\mathbb{Z}$ | 2 | 3 | **Setun** (Brusentsov, MSU, 1959) |
| **2** | $A_2$ hex | **6** | **7** | **GQ128** (128 bits) |
| 3 | FCC $A_3$ | 12 | 13 | G626 (626 bits) |
| 8 | $E_8$ | 240 | 241 | — (Viazovska, 2016) |
| 24 | Leech $\Lambda_{24}$ | 196,560 | 196,561 | — (Cohn–Kumar–Miller–Radchenko–Viazovska, 2016) |

**GQ128 is the only member of the family that fits entirely in a standard 128-bit register.** And the only one whose arithmetic operations are all $O(1)$ on a suitable AXR architecture.
